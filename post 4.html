"Syrupy Gulab Jamun : A Sweet Symphony"

23'rd May 2023

Welcome to my kitchen, where the air is filled with the enticing aroma of cardamom and rosewater. Today, I'm taking you on a journey to create the epitome of Indian sweetness – homemade Gulab Jamun. Get ready to dive into a world of soft, melt-in-your-mouth goodness that's surprisingly easy to achieve from the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients


• 1 cup milk powder • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter) • A pinch of baking soda • 2-3 tablespoons milk (as needed) • Vegetable oil for deep frying
For the Sugar Syrup: • 1 cup sugar • 1/2 cup water • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder • A splash of rosewater (optional)


Prepare the Dough


In a mixing bowl, combine the milk powder, all-purpose flour, ghee, and a pinch of baking soda. Slowly add milk, one tablespoon at a time, and knead the mixture into a soft, smooth dough. The key is to achieve a consistency that's not too dry or too sticky. If needed, adjust by adding a bit more milk. Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls without any cracks. Ensure they are uniform in size for even cooking. The size is a personal preference; some love bite-sized, while others prefer slightly larger ones.

Heat the Oil


In a deep pan, heat enough vegetable oil for deep frying. To check if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of dough into it – if it sizzles and rises to the top, your oil is ready. Gently slide the prepared jamuns into the hot oil, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them on low to medium heat until they achieve a golden brown color. The slow frying is crucial to ensure they cook evenly from the inside.


Prepare the Sugar Syrup


While the jamuns are frying, let's prepare the sugar syrup. In a separate saucepan, combine sugar, water, and cardamom powder. Bring it to a boil and then let it simmer for 5-7 minutes until it reaches a slightly sticky consistency. Add a splash of rosewater if you like that extra floral note. Once the jamuns are golden brown, carefully transfer them to the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least an hour, allowing the syrup to penetrate and infuse every inch of these delectable treats.

And there you have it – homemade Gulab Jamuns that rival any you'd find at your favorite Indian sweet shop. Enjoy these sweet delights, and savor the joy of creating something so indulgent from scratch. Until next time, happy cooking!
